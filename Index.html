Parts of Speech

Noun

Definition: A word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

Kinds of Nouns:

  • Common Noun: General names for a person, place, thing, or idea.
    • dog
    • city
    • car
    • book
    • teacher
  • Proper Noun: Specific names of people, places, or organizations, capitalized.
    • London
    • Alice
    • Toyota
    • Amazon
    • Eiffel Tower
  • Abstract Noun: Names an idea, quality, or state rather than a concrete object.
    • beauty
    • courage
    • wisdom
    • freedom
    • love
  • Collective Noun: Names a group of people or things.
    • team
    • flock
    • committee
    • family
    • audience
  • Concrete Noun: Names things that you can see, hear, smell, taste, or touch.
    • cat
    • cake
    • chair
    • apple
    • computer

Pronoun

Definition: A word that takes the place of a noun.

Kinds of Pronouns:

  • Personal Pronoun: Refers to a specific person or thing and changes form to indicate person, number, gender, and case.
    • I
    • you
    • he
    • she
    • they
  • Possessive Pronoun: Indicates ownership.
    • mine
    • yours
    • theirs
    • his
    • hers
  • Reflexive Pronoun: Refers back to the subject of the sentence.
    • myself
    • yourself
    • himself
    • herself
    • themselves
  • Demonstrative Pronoun: Points out a specific person, place, thing, or idea.
    • this
    • that
    • these
    • those
  • Interrogative Pronoun: Used to ask questions.
    • who
    • what
    • which
    • whom
    • whose

Verb

Definition: A word that expresses action or state of being.

Kinds of Verbs:

  • Action Verb: Expresses physical or mental action.
    • run
    • jump
    • think
    • write
    • sleep
  • Linking Verb: Connects the subject with a word that gives information about the subject, such as a condition or relationship.
    • is
    • are
    • seem
    • become
    • appear
  • Helping Verb: Helps the main verb express action or a state of being.
    • will
    • have
    • can
    • may
    • should
  • Transitive Verb: An action verb that requires a direct object to complete its meaning.
    • carry
    • hit
    • throw
    • build
    • read
  • Intransitive Verb: An action verb that does not require a direct object.
    • sleep
    • die
    • arrive
    • laugh
    • swim

Adjective

Definition: A word that describes a noun or pronoun.

Kinds of Adjectives:

  • Descriptive Adjective: Describes a quality or characteristic of a noun.
    • blue
    • large
    • pretty
    • happy
    • quick
  • Quantitative Adjective: Indicates the quantity of a noun.
    • some
    • many
    • few
    • several
    • two
  • Demonstrative Adjective: Points out which noun is being referred to.
    • this
    • that
    • these
    • those
  • Possessive Adjective: Shows ownership or possession.
    • my
    • your
    • their
    • his
    • her
  • Interrogative Adjective: Used to ask questions about nouns.
    • which
    • what
    • whose
    • how many
    • how much

Adverb

Definition: A word that modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

Kinds of Adverbs:

  • Adverb of Manner: Describes how an action is performed.
    • quickly
    • slowly
    • carefully
    • happily
    • sadly
  • Adverb of Time: Indicates when an action occurs.
    • now
    • then
    • soon
    • yesterday
    • always
  • Adverb of Place: Indicates where an action occurs.
    • here
    • there
    • everywhere
    • nowhere
    • inside
  • Adverb of Frequency: Indicates how often an action occurs.
    • always
    • never
    • often
    • rarely
    • sometimes
  • Adverb of Degree: Indicates the intensity or degree of an action, an adjective, or another adverb.
    • very
    • quite
    • almost
    • too
    • enough

Preposition

Definition: A word that shows the relationship of a noun or pronoun to another word.

Kinds of Prepositions:

  • Preposition of Place: Shows the location or position of something.
    • in
    • on
    • under
    • above
    • below
  • Preposition of Time: Indicates when something happens.
    • at
    • during
    • before
    • after
    • since
  • Preposition of Direction: Shows the direction of movement.
    • to
    • towards
    • into
    • onto
    • through
  • Preposition of Agent: Indicates the agent or the doer of the action.
    • by
    • with
    • from
    • of
    • for
  • Preposition of Instrument: Indicates the instrument or means by which an action is performed.
    • with
    • by
    • using
    • through
    • via

Conjunction

Definition: A word that joins words, phrases, or clauses.

Kinds of Conjunctions:

  • Coordinating Conjunction: Joins words, phrases, or clauses of equal importance.
    • and
    • but
    • or
    • nor
    • for
  • Subordinating Conjunction: Joins a subordinate clause to a main clause.
    • because
    • although
    • if
    • when
    • since
  • Correlative Conjunction: Pairs of conjunctions that work together.
    • either...or
    • neither...nor
    • both...and
    • not only...but also
    • whether...or
  • Conjunctive Adverb: Connects independent clauses and indicates the relationship between them.
    • however
    • therefore
    • moreover
    • consequently
    • nevertheless
  • Adverbial Conjunction: An adverb that functions like a conjunction by connecting clauses.
    • hence
    • thus
    • still
    • accordingly
    • otherwise

Interjection

Definition: A word or phrase that expresses strong emotion or surprise.

Kinds of Interjections:

  • Interjections of Greeting: Used to greet someone.
    • hello
    • hi
    • hey
    • howdy
    • welcome
  • Interjections of Joy: Expresses happiness or joy.
    • hooray
    • yay
    • wow
    • yippee
    • fantastic
  • Interjections of Surprise: Expresses surprise or shock.
    • wow
    • oh
    • ah
    • gosh
    • yikes
  • Interjections of Sorrow: Expresses sadness or regret.
    • alas
    • oh no
    • ouch
    • sigh
    • oh dear
  • Interjections of Attention: Used to get someone's attention.
    • listen
    • hey
    • look
    • behold
    • observe
